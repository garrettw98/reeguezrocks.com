Camp Tahquitz is a 640-acre private camp in the San Bernardino National Forest, near Angelus Oaks. About 90 minutes from Los Angeles via Highway 38.

  • From LA: Take I-10 East to Highway 38 (near Redlands). Follow 38 through the mountains for approx. 30 miles.
  • Check-in: Valid Government ID (18+) and your ticket barcode (digital or printed) are required for entry.
  • Arrival Window: Gates open at 10 AM Thursday (12 PM Wednesday for Early Arrival). Re-entry allowed from 10 AM - Midnight.

Camping & Stay

  • GA Parking + Walk-in Camping — Included with every festival pass. Park in the designated lot and walk to your forest campsite.
  • Car Camping — $40 add-on. Park your vehicle directly at your campsite in The Meadows. Assigned spots. Limited to 200 passes.
  • RV Camping (Dry) — $100 add-on. Designated RV spots with no hookups. Limited to 10 spots.
  • Glamping Package — $500 (2 people). Includes car parking next to setup, 4-person tent with full bed/linens, EZ-Up shade, cooler with daily free ice, and access to nearby showers/bathrooms. Unlimited availability.
  • Historic Cabins — $1,000 Flat Rate. Wood Burning Stove heat (BYO wood). Flexible occupancy (10 beds + floor space). Bathrooms & showers nearby. Only 6 available.
  • Sound Camps — We encourage sound camps! However, all systems must be battery and solar powered only. No fuel generators are permitted anywhere on site.
What is Reeguez Rocks 2026?

Reeguez Rocks is a community-driven bass and dubstep music festival entering its sixth year. We are an intimate 499-capacity event, where every ticket sold directly increases the production quality and artist lineup.

What are the stages like?

The Bowl serves as our high-production Main Stage, nestled in a natural amphitheater. The Dome is our 28-foot geodesic chill sanctuary, offering an intimate late-night experience with a live DJ and seating. No massive LED walls here—just high-fidelity sound and nature.

Can I bring my own sound system?

Yes! Sound camps are highly encouraged. However, the venue has a strict No Generator policy. All systems must be powered by batteries and/or solar panels.

What is the age requirement?

18+ only. Valid government-issued ID required at check-in.

What is the refund policy?

Before Tier 1 Goal is Met: If we do not hit our "Event Confirmed" sales goal, all backers will receive a full refund automatically.
After Tier 1: All sales final. Transfers are allowed.

What are the venue rules?

Strictly prohibited: Glass, weapons, fuel generators, and illegal items. NO OPEN FIRES or Campfires anywhere on property. The only permitted flames are inside the wood-burning stoves within the Historic Cabins. Cooking stoves must be propane/gas only.

What should I bring?

Bring a tent, sleeping bag, very warm layers (nights in October at 6,500ft can be very cold), headlamp, reusable water bottle, and trash bags. We are a Leave No Trace event.

Is there cell service?

Signal is extremely limited. We will have Starlink for gate operations, but expect to be offline.

Can I leave and re-enter?

Yes, but gates close at Midnight each night and reopen at 10 AM. Please plan your town runs accordingly.

Can I bring my own alcohol?

Yes, BYOB is permitted for campsites (glass ban enforced). Alcohol will also be sold on-site at designated bars.
